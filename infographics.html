DNSSEC: Forging a Chain of Trust

DNSSEC adds cryptographic trust to DNS, verifying that data is authentic and has not been tampered with. It uses four key record types to form an unbreakable chain of trust from the root zone down to a specific domain.

DNSKEY

Contains the public key used to verify digital signatures.

DS Record

"Delegation Signer" - links a child zone's key to its parent, forming the chain.

RRSIG

The actual digital signature for a set of DNS records.

NSEC/NSEC3

Cryptographically proves that a requested domain name does not exist.

Cyber Monk: Making Trust Visible

I built Cyber Monk, a command-line tool that validates and visualizes DNSSEC trust chains. It makes complex cryptographic validation accessible, educational, and reproducible for everyone from students to sysadmins.
